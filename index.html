The genome promised us everything. It delivered half.

For decades, we've sequenced genomes and measured transcripts, believing RNA would tell us everything about cellular function. It doesn't.

Proteins are the true executors of life. They fold, they modify, they interact, they degradeall beyond the reach of transcriptomics. GWAS studies identify thousands of disease-associated variants, yet most remain mechanistically unexplained.

Genoproteomics bridges this gap. We systematically map how every genetic variant shapes the proteomeits structure, modifications, interactions, and dynamics.

Not predictions. Not proxies. Direct, measurable, actionable protein phenotypes.

GP
Our Mission

To build the complete map from genetic variation to protein phenotypeenabling precision medicine that actually reaches the molecules that matter.

Most drugs target proteins, not RNA. Most cellular functions are executed by proteins, not RNA. It's time our understanding of genetic variation caught up with this reality.

Kushal Raj Roy, Founder

Our Framework

From Sequence to Function

A systematic four-step approach to variant-proteotype mapping.

1

Variant Curation

Curate disease and population variants from GWAS, ClinVar, and gnomAD with protein-level annotations.

2

Structure Modeling

Predict variant impact on 3D structure using AlphaFold, MD simulations, and free energy calculations.

3

Experimental Validation

Validate predictions with HDX-MS, thermal stability assays, and protein-protein interaction mapping.

4

Mechanistic Models

Build quantitative models linking variants to proteome phenotypes: abundance, PTMs, localization.

Leadership

Kushal Raj Roy

A computational structural biologist pioneering the integration of genetic variation with protein structure and function. With expertise spanning molecular dynamics simulations, AlphaFold predictions, and mass spectrometry-based proteomics.

His research focuses on translational GTPases and ribosomal translation mechanismsthe molecular machines that convert genetic information into proteins. This work revealed how subtle sequence variations propagate through protein structure to alter cellular function.

Genoproteomics emerged from a fundamental insight: we have the tools to connect every genetic variant to its protein-level consequences. The field just needs to be built.
